Brno Zoo

Open since 1953

Brno Zoo has been operating for over 60 years. Located in the Mniší hora area in Brno-Bystrc, it covers more than 65 hectares. It’s known for the natural breeding of polar bears and the endangered Hispaniolan solenodon.

The Beringia exhibit, representing the region between Siberia and Alaska, features Kamchatka brown bears, arctic foxes, and wolverines. Other highlights include the Congo lion enclosure, bald eagle aviary, kangaroo walk-through, and African Village with giraffes, zebras, and antelopes.

The Children's Zoo offers direct contact with animals and play areas. In the Tropical Kingdom pavilion, you’ll find piranhas, turtles, iguanas, sand cats, and the rare Komodo dragon.

The zoo also emphasizes ecological education, offering programs and excursions for schools and groups.
